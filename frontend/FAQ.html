Everything you need to know about donating blood and using LifeDrop to connect with patients.

How often can I donate blood?

You can donate whole blood every 90 days (3 months). Platelet donations can be made every 7 days, up to 24 times per year. Always wait the full recommended period to allow your body to fully recover.

How long does a donation take?

The actual blood draw takes about 8–10 minutes. Including registration, a health screening, and a short rest period, the whole process takes around 45–60 minutes from start to finish.

What should I eat before donating?

Drink plenty of water and eat a healthy, non-fatty meal 2–3 hours before your appointment.

How does LifeDrop match donors with patients?

LifeDrop uses your blood type, location, and availability to match you with patients in need. When a patient matching your profile posts a request, you receive a notification so you can respond immediately.

Is LifeDrop free to use?

Yes, LifeDrop is completely free for both donors and patients. Our mission is to make blood donation as accessible as possible.
